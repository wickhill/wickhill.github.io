About Me:

Hi General Assembly!  My name is William "Wick" Hill.  I live just outside of Boston, and my interest in coding goes back to high school, which is around the time I began programming my TI-83 to solve long-form math and science equations.

I've worked in several different fields during my life. I worked for a small software company based out of Portland, Oregon called I.S.I.  I've always thought it was really cool that they were founded way back in 1988!  (I came along much later though, haha.)  I also worked as a paralegal for a firm based in NYC that specializes in International Child Custody.  My bosses, Bob Arenstein and Sandra Nu√±ez (seen in the image to the right), even won a case before The Hague!  Though again, that was before my time.  Sandra and Bob taught me a lot, and many of the cases we worked on together were pretty intense, but Bob and Sandra always made sure that we had fun. It was a pretty incredible experience!

I left Arenstein Law and decided to focus my efforts on music full-time.  Music is one of my passions, and it was another industry I'd been a part of from time to time, having worked as an Assistant Engineer in various studios.  I put into practice what I'd learned from those experiences and built my own recording spaces where I could engineer my albums. (It's much cheaper that way.) Speaking of cheap: because I needed to live frugally, the studios I built were sometimes in remote locations. Check out my driveway, my car, and my neighbor's house in the picture in the upper-left!  (FYI: Winters in Maine are cold!)  When I'd finished tracking an album I'd hop over to a studio where I knew a professional Mixing Engineer, and then voila!  Album done.  (In the picture to the right I'm peering over the shoulder of the one and only Brian Charles.  He's the head engineer over at the very fine Zippah Studios, and I happen to think that he's the best Mixing Engineer in Boston!)

All in all I've released four full-length albums and an EP, and some of my tunes can be found here!

Now I'm putting music on hold while I pursue coding.  I have friends and family who've taken the coding route, and whether they're former-musicians themselves or whether they came to it from another profession there's one thing that unites them all: they all love what they're doing now with coding!



I've really enjoyed meeting people from General Assembly: everybody's passion for Learning and Collaboration has been very inspiring.  And that goes for both teachers and potential students alike!  Here's looking forward to speaking further about General Assembly and learning more about the possibilities offered by the world coding. Cheers!
